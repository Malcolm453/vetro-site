Our work

Adding personality to flavour

Tic Tac

We had a lot of fun helping Cotlands, a non-profit that focuses on early childhood development and the power of play, with their TVC for DStv. The campaign followed the lines of the traditional hero concept, where the protagonist of the story transitions from an ordinary life to an extraordinary one. The question that needed answering, was how to showcase this transition visually at the key moment when the protagonists’ life changes, without having to tell the viewer. By a simple metamorphosis from dull tones and a watered down background, to bright, colourful and powerful tones, we successfully showcased the life-altering change from a visual perspective, merging the script and scene to create something truly magical.

The art of animation in creating an immersive brand experience

King Pie

As lead agency for King Pie, we had the fantastic opportunity to bring their one of their digital competitions to life through the animated, spy-enthralled adventures of the King Pie man in the SPIE Adventures. This carefully curated 2-D animated series of four episodes saw the King Pie man embark on various activities, inviting brand fans to watch the episodes, look for clues and stand a chance to win awesome prizes. This project really set the bar for how we transformed social experiences for followers via animation on the brand’s various platforms.

The “sign” in “design”: Semiotics and their importance to design

Learn

Semiotics isn’t just reserved for academics – we designers practice semiotics every day and understanding the power of signs and their meanings, helps us effectively translate our ideas something that is truly impactful and meaningful. It is always important to not just think about what we are putting on a blank canvass from a visual perspective but how this visual piece of work is signified.

Design has to be driven by purpose

Implement

Like putting the right words to paper, a designer’s piece of work is just as important as the content it represents. Intention is the elixir of life for design. Sure, you can add pretty images to pretty designs, and make it look spectacular and inviting, but what does it actually represent to its intended audience? When we take a step back and recognise that designing with a solid goal and purpose is something to appreciate and incorporate, do we truly transform our visual content into a multi-dimensional experience, one that illustrates purpose, passion and meaning.

Great design DOES lead to great ROI

Achieve

In agency life, we appreciate that the human mind is both beautiful and mysterious. It’s attracted to the aesthetics that surrounds it in that moment, while reading into the surrounding’s meaning without us even consciously acknowledging it at that exact moment. This always reminds us as designers, that great design is one of the best investments a brand can make when looking for consequential ROI. Taking into consideration the “laws of attraction” and what we respond to as humans, we take head in the fact that a powerful design or image can really impact the way someone responds or engages with a brand
